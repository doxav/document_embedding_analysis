The resource management layer coordinates actions among compute and storage nodes and manages resource allocation and scheduling in distributed systems to enable parallel processing of high volume and velocity of data streams  . A typical data stream cluster consists of several compute nodes, and a cluster manager, which coordinates communication between the nodes  . Processes in a distributed system can either exchange messages directly through a network or read/write into some shared storage. Coordination tasks such as electing a master node, managing group memberships, and managing metadata are important when building a multi-cluster distributed system BIB63421046 .
