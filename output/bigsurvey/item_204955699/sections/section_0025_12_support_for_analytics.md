Besides the regular search and data storage systems, another desirable feature of DSPEs is support for analytical and continuous queries. The usual pipeline for mining and modelling data involves taking a sample or a snapshot from production data, cleaning and pre-processing the data, training and evaluating a model, and finally deploying the model to a production system. Most implementations of this traditional data mining pipeline such as WEKA and R are unable to cope with web-scale datasets. Hence the evolution of distributed data mining systems such as Mahout  . However, most data are now generated in the form of a stream and requires streaming models BIB13258458 . This involves analyzing data in near real-time or as soon as it arrives into the system. Most implementations of traditional stream processing systems such as Massive Online Analysis (MOA) are limited by the memory and bandwidth of a single machine BIB18216402 . Hence the evolution of distributed data stream mining systems such as the Scalable Advanced Massive Online Analysis (SAMOA), which enables the development of distributed streaming algorithms and executing them on multiple DSPEs BIB18216402 .
