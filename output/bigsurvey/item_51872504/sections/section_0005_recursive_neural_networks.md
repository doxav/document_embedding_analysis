Another notable type of artificial neural network used in natural language processing is the recursive neural network (RvNN) BIB6536466 BIB20186027 . Much like convolutional networks, recursive networks use a form of weight sharing to minimize training. However, whereas CNNs share weights horizontally (within a layer), recursive nets share weights vertically (between layers). This is particularly appealing in NLP, as it allows for easy modeling of structures such as parse trees. In recursive networks, a single tensor (or a generalized matrix) of weights can be used at a low level in the tree, and then used recursively at successively higher levels of the tree BIB6979578 . Since nodes in RvNNs are dependent on their previous results, and therefore feed back to themselves, RvNNs are not considered to be feedforward.
