Most SMT-solvers follow the DPLL(T) architecture: a solver for pure propositional formulas, following the DPLL or CDCL class of algorithms, drives decision procedures for each theory (e.g. linear arithmetic) by adding or retracting constraints and querying for satisfiability. DPLL(T) and decision procedures for many interesting logics are explained in more detail in e.g. BIB52887640  .
