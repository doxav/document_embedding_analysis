The simplest way to evaluate a summary is to have a human assess its quality. For example, in DUC, the judges would evaluate the coverage of the summary, i.e. how much the candidate summary covered the original given input. In more recent paradigms, in particular TAC, query-based summaries have been created. Then judges evaluate to what extent a summary answers the given query. The factors that human experts must consider when giving scores to each candidate summary are grammaticality, non redundancy, integration of most important pieces of information, structure and coherence.
