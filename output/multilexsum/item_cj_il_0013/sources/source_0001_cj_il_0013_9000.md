104 MINUTE entry before the Honorable Virginia M. Kendall. Class member Christopher C. Parker filed what he styled as a "motion to intervene" 100 emphasizing a legal argument regarding the State's sentencing scheme. Mr. Parker is already a party in this case (at least for the substantive basis of this motion, arguing the law) and the purpose of intervention is to become a party, so the motion is improper. See Kifer v. Ellsworth, 346 F.3d 1155, 1156 (7th Cir. 2003). Additionally, the Court cannot construe the motion as one made to opt out of the class because there is no right to opt out of a Rule 23(b)(2) class action, and in the Court's discretion, doing so here is unnecessary anyway. See id. at 1157; see also Gray v. Hardy, 826 F.3d 1000, 1009 (7th Cir. 2016). The existing parties adequately represent Mr. Parker's interests, and he does not contend that there are any factual or legal distinctions that make his situation different from that of the named class representatives. See Zbaraz v. Madigan, 572 F.3d 370, 377 (7th Cir. 2009); see also Crawford v. Equifax Payment Servs., Inc., 201 F.3d 877, 880 (7th Cir. 2000) (stating that the only time a class member should need to intervene is if she thinks that the representative is not acting in her best interests). In fact, the point that Mr. Parker raises in his motion is consistent with the plaintiff's position in this case. Should Mr. Parker want to individually sue (for money damages) in the future, he will likely be able to do so. See Cole v. City of Memphis, 839 F.3d 530, 541 (6th Cir. 2016), cert. denied sub nom. City of Memphis, Tenn. v. Cole, 137 S. Ct. 2220 (2017); Berry v. Schulman, 807 F.3d 600, 61112 (4th Cir. 2015). But for now, the operating assumption is that "because the relief sought is uniform, so are the interests of the class members..." Berry, 807 F.3d at 612. So the Court decided when it certified the class. See Fed. R. Civ. P. 23 (requiring the court to find that the interests of all of the class members will be fairly and adequately represented by the named plaintiffs and class counsel). Mr. Parker failed to allege facts that might change this conclusion. The Court accordingly denies his motion to intervene 100 .Mailed notice (lk, ) (Entered: 12/20/2018)
